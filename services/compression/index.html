Medical Compression Management

Therapeutic compression for lymphatic and circulatory support

What is it?

Compression garments are therapeutic devices designed to apply graduated pressure to improve circulation, support the lymphatic system, and manage fluid build-up. They are medically prescribed and individually measured for optimal therapeutic effect.

How can it help?

  • Reducing and preventing swelling
  • Supporting lymphatic drainage
  • Improving venous return
  • Preventing fluid accumulation
  • Supporting wound and ulcer healing
  • Softening fibrotic tissue
  • Managing post-operative oedema
  • Reducing leg fatigue and discomfort
  • Improving recovery after sport or travel

Devices We Provide

A selection of the devices we fit within this service. Filter by area of the body to find what fits your needs — the right device is always confirmed after an individual assessment.

Lower Limb

Below-Knee Compression Stocking

Daily graduated compression for patients with lower-leg swelling, varicose veins or venous insufficiency.

A below-knee compression stocking applies graduated pressure that is firmest at the ankle and gradually decreases up the calf, supporting venous and lymphatic return in the lower leg. It is fitted to individual leg measurements and a prescribed compression class for everyday wear.

  • Graduated compression profile, firmest at the ankle
  • Available in multiple compression classes
  • Fitted to individual leg measurements
  • Breathable, durable knitted fabric
Lower Limb

Thigh-High Compression Stocking

Graduated compression for patients whose swelling or venous symptoms extend above the knee.

A thigh-high compression stocking extends graduated compression from the ankle to the thigh, giving wider coverage than a below-knee stocking for venous and lymphatic conditions affecting the whole leg. It is fitted to the patient's individual leg measurements and includes a top band designed to help keep the stocking in place.

  • Extended graduated compression to the thigh
  • Silicone or fabric top band to reduce slipping
  • Fitted to individual leg measurements
  • Open- or closed-toe options available
Lower Limb

Compression Tights

Full-leg graduated compression in a single garment, including a pregnancy-adapted option.

Compression tights deliver graduated compression across both legs in one waist-height garment, offering an alternative to wearing two separate stockings. A pregnancy-specific style accommodates changes in abdominal size while continuing to support circulation in the legs.

  • Full-leg graduated compression in a single garment
  • Waist-height design without a separate band
  • Pregnancy-adapted styles available
  • Fitted to individual measurements
Upper Limb

Compression Arm Sleeve

Graduated arm compression for patients managing lymphoedema, most often after breast cancer treatment or lymph node removal.

A compression arm sleeve applies graduated pressure along the arm to support lymphatic drainage and help control or reduce swelling. It is available in below- or above-elbow lengths, fitted to individual arm measurements, and can be paired with a separate hand gauntlet for combined coverage.

  • Graduated compression along the arm
  • Below-elbow or above-elbow length options
  • Fitted to individual arm measurements
  • Can be combined with a separate hand gauntlet
Wrist & Hand

Compression Glove & Gauntlet

Graduated hand and finger compression, often worn alongside an arm sleeve for combined lymphoedema management.

A compression glove or gauntlet applies graduated pressure across the hand and fingers to help manage swelling, typically worn together with a compression arm sleeve for combined upper-limb coverage. It is fitted to the individual's hand measurements and made from breathable knitted fabric for extended daily wear.

  • Graduated compression across the hand and fingers
  • Available with or without individual finger coverage
  • Designed to pair with a matching arm sleeve
  • Fitted to individual hand measurements
Chest

Post-Mastectomy Compression Garment

Chest-area compression support for patients recovering from mastectomy or breast surgery.

A post-mastectomy compression garment applies gentle, even pressure across the chest to support healing tissue, help manage swelling and improve comfort following breast surgery. It is fitted to individual chest measurements, with soft, seamless construction and adjustable or front-closure designs suited to early post-operative wear.

  • Even compression across the chest wall
  • Soft, seamless construction against healing skin
  • Adjustable or front-closure designs for easy wear
  • Fitted to individual chest measurements
Full Body

Scar & Burn Compression Garment

Sustained compression over a healing area for patients recovering from significant burns or scarring.

A scar or burn compression garment applies steady, even pressure to a healing area of skin and is commonly used to support management of scar tissue texture and appearance after a burn or major wound. It is custom-fitted to the specific affected body region, from a single limb to a larger area, and refitted periodically as healing progresses.

  • Even, sustained compression over the healing area
  • Custom-fitted to the specific affected body region
  • Breathable, durable compression fabric
  • Refitted periodically as healing progresses
Lower Limb

Multi-Layer Compression Bandaging System

Higher, clinician-applied compression for more severe swelling or venous/lymphatic conditions, often before transitioning to a garment.

A multi-layer compression bandaging system combines a protective padding layer with elastic or short-stretch bandage layers, applied by a trained clinician to deliver higher and more controllable compression than a made-to-measure garment. It is typically used during an initial intensive treatment phase for significant swelling, before a patient transitions to a standard compression garment.

  • Multiple layers for controlled, higher compression
  • Padding layer protects skin and evens pressure
  • Applied and reapplied by a trained clinician
  • Used during intensive-phase swelling reduction

Understanding Compression

Compression Dosage (mmHg)

Compression strength is measured in millimeters of mercury (mmHg). The appropriate dosage depends on your condition, tissue health, and treatment goals.

Stiffness and Elasticity

Garments vary in stiffness. Stiffer garments provide more support during movement, while elastic garments are easier to apply and suitable for maintenance therapy.

Garment Types

Flat-knit garments are custom-made for complex shapes. Circular-knit garments are ready-to-wear and suitable for mild to moderate swelling. Night garments and wraps offer alternative support options.

Our Clinical Approach

Compression therapy requires careful prescription. We conduct thorough assessment to determine the appropriate compression dosage (mmHg), stiffness, garment type, textile, and material. Garments are measured and fitted in practice, home, or hospital settings as needed.
